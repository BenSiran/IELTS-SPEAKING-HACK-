Part 2: Individual Long Turn

Part 2 lasts 3-4 minutes. You'll receive a task card and have 1 minute to prepare, then speak for 2 minutes.

üéØ Cue Card Topic 1
Describe a person who has influenced you

You should say:
‚Ä¢ Who this person is
‚Ä¢ How you know them
‚Ä¢ What influence they have had on you
‚Ä¢ And explain why this person is important to you
SAMPLE ANSWER (2 MINUTES):

I'd like to talk about my high school mathematics teacher, Mrs. Patterson, who has had a profound influence on my life and career path.

I first met Mrs. Patterson when I was 15 years old and struggling with advanced mathematics. Unlike other teachers, she didn't just teach formulas; she showed us the beauty and logic behind mathematical concepts. She had this incredible ability to break down complex problems into manageable steps and always encouraged us to think critically rather than memorize solutions.

Her influence on me has been multifaceted. Academically, she transformed my relationship with mathematics from one of fear and confusion to genuine interest and confidence. She spent countless hours after school helping me and other students, never making us feel like we were a burden. But more importantly, she taught me the value of perseverance. Whenever I wanted to give up on a difficult problem, she'd say, "The struggle is where the learning happens."

This mindset has stayed with me throughout my university studies and continues to guide me today. When I face challenges in my computer science courses, I hear her voice encouraging me to persist. She also inspired my interest in helping others learn, and I now volunteer as a tutor myself, trying to pass on the same patience and enthusiasm she showed me.

Mrs. Patterson is important to me because she saw potential in me that I didn't see in myself. She believed in my abilities even when I doubted them, and that belief gave me the confidence to pursue a challenging field of study. She wasn't just a teacher; she was a mentor who genuinely cared about her students' success beyond the classroom.

üéØ Cue Card Topic 2
Describe a memorable journey you have made

You should say:
‚Ä¢ Where you went
‚Ä¢ Who you went with
‚Ä¢ What you did there
‚Ä¢ And explain why this journey was memorable
SAMPLE ANSWER (2 MINUTES):

I'd like to share my experience of a backpacking trip I took to the mountains last summer, which turned out to be one of the most memorable journeys of my life.

My destination was a remote mountain region in the northern highlands, known for its pristine natural beauty and challenging hiking trails. I went with three close friends from university, all of whom shared my passion for outdoor adventures. We had been planning this trip for months, carefully researching routes and preparing our equipment.

During our five-day expedition, we hiked through dense forests, crossed crystal-clear streams, and camped under star-filled skies. One of the highlights was reaching a summit at sunrise on our third day ‚Äì the view was absolutely breathtaking, with clouds below us and snow-capped peaks stretching endlessly in every direction. We also encountered local villagers who were incredibly hospitable, sharing meals with us and telling stories about the region's history and wildlife.

What made this journey particularly memorable was the combination of physical challenge and personal growth. There were moments when I doubted whether I could continue, especially during steep climbs with a heavy backpack. But pushing through these difficulties with the support of my friends taught me valuable lessons about resilience and teamwork. We had to rely on each other completely ‚Äì whether it was navigating trails, setting up camp, or simply keeping spirits high when conditions were tough.

This journey was transformative because it took me completely out of my comfort zone and away from the distractions of modern life. Without phone signal or internet, we were fully present in each moment. The experience deepened my friendships and gave me a profound appreciation for nature's beauty and power. Even now, months later, I find myself reflecting on that trip whenever I face challenges in daily life.

Describe a skill you would like to learn

You should say:
‚Ä¢ What the skill is
‚Ä¢ Why you want to learn it
‚Ä¢ How you plan to learn it
‚Ä¢ And explain how this skill would benefit you
SAMPLE ANSWER (2 MINUTES):

The skill I'm really eager to learn is playing the piano. It's something I've been fascinated by for years but never had the opportunity to pursue properly.

My interest in piano began when I attended a classical music concert a few years ago. Watching the pianist's fingers dance across the keys while creating such beautiful, complex melodies was mesmerizing. Since then, I've realized that learning piano isn't just about music ‚Äì it's about discipline, coordination, and expressing emotions in a unique way. I'm also drawn to the cognitive benefits; research shows that playing piano enhances memory, concentration, and multitasking abilities.

As for my learning plan, I've already started doing some groundwork. I've been watching online tutorials to understand basic music theory and finger positioning. However, I recognize that to progress seriously, I'll need structured lessons from a qualified instructor. I'm planning to enroll in weekly classes starting next month. I've also downloaded several practice apps that gamify learning, which should make daily practice more engaging. My goal is to dedicate at least 30 minutes every day to practice, gradually building up my skills.

The benefits of learning piano would be substantial. On a personal level, it would provide a creative outlet and a way to relax after stressful days. Music has always been therapeutic for me, and creating it myself would be even more rewarding. Professionally, the discipline and perseverance required to master an instrument would undoubtedly benefit my career.
